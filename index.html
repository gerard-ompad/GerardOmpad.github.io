Detecting Diseases in Corn through Convolutional Neural Network Architectures and Ensemble
James Vincent Bacus, Caitlin Mariel Lindsay, Christian Maderazo, Gerard Ompad
AICCC 2024: 2024 the 7th Artificial Intelligence and Cloud Computing Conference, 2023

Implemented a pipeline that combines data augmentation, transfer learning on three convolutional neural networks architectures (DenseNet201, EfficientNetV2M, and InceptionResNetV2), and a soft voting ensemble to improve classification performance for corn leaf diseases.

Investigating Causal Relationships Between Inflation News Among Other News Topics In Philippine News Media Using Granger Causality
Erwin Antepuesto, Stan Kiefer Gallego, Gerard Ompad, Angie Ceniza
2024 IEEE International Conference on Communication, Networks and Satellite (COMNETSAT), 2024

Beyond economic concerns, inflation has also influenced the media narratives of other topics. This study aims to explore how inflation news impacts various aspects of Philippine society by investigating the relationships of inflation-related news articles and other news topics using Granger Causality.

Multi-Network Based Approach for Drug Repurposing
Ronan Jasper G. Reponte, Joshua Rodriguez, Gerard Ompad, Angie Ceniza
2024 IEEE International Conference on Communication, Networks and Satellite (COMNETSAT), 2024

Utilizes a novel complex multi-layered network approach for aggregating drug networks of varying information into a single unified network. Under the hypothesis that drugs that are closely related will appear as neighboring nodes, using a community detection approach allows novel discovery of new applications of old and existing drugs. This paper describesa method to aggregate multi-layered network. Drug data is collated from DrugBank and PubChem; an adjacency matrix was created, fromw hich the Normalized Graph Laplacian is generated. Prior to network aggregation, eigenvectors, and Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection (UMAP) methods were utilized to reduce the network dimension. Additionally, an inference was implemented to test for completespatial randomness (CSR) to check for node scattering.
